How to convert .XMP to .lrtempalte yourself?

Starting with Lightroom 7.3, the way you save and manage presets has changed. Lightroom versions 7.3 and higher can only accept and read .lrtempaltes.

The only way at the moment to convert .xmp to .lrtempalte on your own consists of the following:
1. Used 2 versions of Lightroom simultaneously: older than 7.3 and younger than 7.3. As a younger version, it is recommended to use Lightroom generation 4 (for example, Lightroom 4.3).
2. In the older version of Lightroom, you need to export your presets in .dng format.
3. Then load your presets as .dng files into the Lightroom minor version. You can export your presets in .lrtemplate format.

Can you convert .lrtemplate to a .XMP file?

Any new version of Lightroom above 7.3 will accept .lrtemplate files and automatically convert them to an XMP file.
To do this, you need to click File> Import Profiles and Templates to import them later.
After downloading the .lrtempalte files, restart Lightroom, and wait a few seconds. Conversion will happen pretty quickly, and your presets will be converted to .XMP format.
